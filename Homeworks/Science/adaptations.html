Science Homework: Adaptations, Genetics, and Extinction

Question 1: Mammoths and Elephants

Below are images of a mammoth and an elephant. The mammoth and elephant are believed to be closely related.

Reasons for their close relationship:

  • Similar physical features, such as large bodies and tusks.
  • Genetic evidence suggesting a common ancestry.

Mammoths are now extinct. This means they no longer exist in the wild or captivity.

Factors that might lead to extinction:

  • Climate change
  • Habitat loss
  • Human hunting or poaching

Question 2: Identical Twins - Jack and James

Jack and James are identical twins.

Their hair color is identical due to their shared genetic makeup. However, differences in weight can occur due to:

  • Varying diet and nutrition intake
  • Different levels of physical activity
  • Metabolism variations

Question 3: Adaptations and Desert Rats

Animals are adapted to survive in their environment. The desert rat lives in hot, sandy conditions.

Match the following adaptations with their function:

  • Long tail: To help them balance when running.
  • Brown skin: Camouflage from predators.
  • Large feet: To prevent sinking into sand.

Animals such as the desert rat compete with each other to survive. They compete for resources like food and water, as well as mates for reproduction.
